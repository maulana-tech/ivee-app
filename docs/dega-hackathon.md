About DEGA
DEGA is building tools and infrastructure to empower developers to launch AI prediction market automation. With a strong focus on developer experience and community-driven innovation, DEGA simplifies prediction market development through Canon — a CLI toolkit that lets developers bootstrap, configure, and deploy trading strategies from the terminal in minutes.

Core Technology
Participants will leverage:

Canon CLI: The guided entry point for your entire workflow. canon init scaffolds your project from a strategy template, canon start detects your project phase and drives the pipeline automatically — from scaffolding through development to live execution. One command to go from idea to running automations.
TypeScript Strategy Templates: Pre-built code base with typed interfaces for market data fetching, strategy logic, and execution — ready to customize. Templates cover arbitrage scanning, momentum trading, cross-market analysis, and more. We'll go over eaach one of these in our workshops.
AI Agent Workflow: Canon includes specialized AI agents — a market analyst to scan opportunities, a strategy architect to design your approach, a developer agent to help implement and iterate, and a QA agent to validate before you go live. Describe what you want in plain language and the agents guide you through building it.
Automation System View: Live terminal dashboard showing each step of your automation (data fetch → analysis → decision → execution), real-time logs, and status monitoring.

Themes
Build AI powered automations focused on prediction market microstructures:

Arbitrage Detection: Exploit price discrepancies for the same event across different markets or within the same platform.
Cross-Market Analysis: Correlate positions across related markets — when one moves, capitalize on the lag in another.
Speed-Based Opportunity: Act on publicly available statistical data (team records, matchups, injury reports) before market prices adjust.
Innovation:

Innovative: Design your own strategy from scratch. Combine data sources, invent new signals, or create entirely new approaches to prediction market trading that nobody has tried before.
Canon's built-in templates focus on microstructure-based approaches. Participants are free to experiment with any strategy type they want, including news sentiment, opinion-based, or entirely custom approaches.

Submission Requirements
Each submission must include:

Project Description: Strategy approach and intended automation behavior.
Source Code: Publicly accessible on GitHub.
Documentation: Setup instructions and an about section describing the automation's flow.
Demo Video: 3 to 5 minute presentation of your project.
Automation Logs: Execution logs from your runs (captured in .canon/execution/).

Judging Criteria
Innovation and Creativity 25% — Originality of your strategy approach and how you apply Canon's tools to solve prediction market problems.
Technical Execution and Design 30% — Code quality, proper use of Canon's framework and interfaces, test coverage, and clean documentation.
Real World Utility and Impact 30% — How well your automation performs against live prediction markets, including profit %, risk management, and adaptability to changing conditions.
Presentation and Demo 15% — Clarity and quality of your demo video and project documentation.
